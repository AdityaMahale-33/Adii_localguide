Beaches Around Ankola

Most beaches here are calm and less crowded. Good for walks, sunsets and photography.

Secluded
Honey Beach (Honnebail)

Small golden-sand beach surrounded by rocks and greenery. Feels like a private cove, perfect for quiet mornings and evenings.

Best time: Sunrise & sunset
River & Sea
Nadibag Beach

A beautiful spot where a small river meets the Arabian Sea. Wide sand, gentle waves and great views from nearby rocks.

Good for: Photos, long walks
Offbeat
Keni / Belekeri Side

Less commercial and still raw. Ideal if you like silent stretches of sand, rocks and just the sound of waves.

Carry water & snacks – limited shops

Temples & Spiritual Spots

Ankola is also known for old coastal temples with strong local traditions.

Heritage
Shri Aryadurga Temple

Popular coastal temple dedicated to Goddess Aryadurga. Visit in the morning for a peaceful darshan and cool sea breeze.

Dress modestly, remove footwear outside
Kuladevata
Lakshminarayan Mahamaya Temple

Important temple for many local families. Calm courtyard, traditional architecture and regular pujas.

Evening aarti is a good time to visit
Viewpoint
Basakal Gudda / Nandi Point

Small hill point near Ankola with a beautiful view of the town, greenery and distant sea. Nice for a short drive.

Best time: Late afternoon

Local Food to Try in Ankola

Mixture of coastal Konkan flavours and classic Karnataka snacks.

Konkani Fish Curry & Rice

Try at: Small local seafood hotels on highway or near town

Spicy tangy fish curry (often bangda / mackerel) with steamed rice. Coconut, tamarind and local masala give a strong coastal flavour.

Idli, Dosa & Buns Breakfast

Try at: Popular darshinis & family restaurants in town

Soft idlis, crispy dosas, mangalore buns, vada and strong filter coffee – perfect start before you head to the beach.

Veg / Fish Thali

Try at: Highway restaurants & local hotels

Simple homely thali with rice, sambar, sabzi, chutney and sometimes fried fish. Good option if you want to taste everything in one plate.

Getting Around Ankola

Small town – most places are reachable by road within 20–30 minutes.

Transport Options

  • Auto / Rikshaw: Good for short distances inside town. Fix price or ask to use meter.
  • Local Bus: Connects Ankola with nearby places like Gokarna, Kumta, Karwar.
  • Cab / Taxi: Useful if you plan to cover multiple beaches in one day.
  • Own Vehicle / Bike: Best flexibility. Roads are generally good but be careful at night.

Quick Tips for Visitors

  • Carry water and some snacks – many beaches are quiet with few shops.
  • Respect local culture at temples – simple clothes & silence inside.
  • Sun can be strong – cap, sunglasses and sunscreen help a lot.
  • Keep your phone charged; network may be weak near some beaches.
